## Why the user's intuition was right
"noclobber once approved â†’ s_load" is confirmed: the sibling block-table loads that GET
noclobber DO become `s_load` in the same kernel. The prefetch is `s_load`-representable
(refuting the S5 story); it just never gets noclobber approved.

## The fix (implemented in /root/llvm-project)
`AMDGPUMemoryUtils.cpp` `isReallyAClobber(const MemoryLocation &Loc, MemoryDef*, AAResults*)`:
- keep `FenceInst` early-return and the barrier-intrinsic whitelist;
- keep the atomic special case, but pointer-check against `Loc.Ptr` (a synchronizing atomic
  on unrelated memory must stay non-clobbering; `getModRefInfo` would over-report Mod for its
  ordering effects â€” this is why the atomic case is NOT folded into getModRefInfo; caught by
  `noclobber-barrier.ll` regressions during development);
- replace the final `return true` with `return isModSet(AA->getModRefInfo(DefInst, Loc))`.
Signature changed `const Value *Ptr` â†’ `const MemoryLocation &Loc` (caller passes the `Loc`
it already builds); header updated + `class MemoryLocation;` fwd-decl added.
